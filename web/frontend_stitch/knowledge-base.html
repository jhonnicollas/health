Understanding Fasting Glucose (Sinocare M101)

A comprehensive guide to measuring, interpreting, and managing fasting blood glucose levels using the Sinocare M101 monitor.

Definition

Fasting blood glucose measures the amount of sugar (glucose) in your blood after you have not eaten or drunk anything (except water) for at least 8 hours. It is a critical metric for diagnosing and managing diabetes or prediabetes. The Sinocare M101 provides rapid, reliable capillary whole blood testing for home use.

Technical Specifications

Metric Name Fasting Glucose
Unit mg/dL
Device Sinocare M101
Normal Range 70 - 99
warning

Danger Range: < 54 mg/dL or > 250 mg/dL requires immediate attention.

How to Measure Properly

Fig 1: Sinocare M101 Device Setup
  1. 1

    Wash and Dry Hands

    Use warm water and soap. Ensure fingers are completely dry before proceeding to avoid diluting the blood sample.

  2. 2

    Prepare the Lancing Device

    Insert a new sterile lancet. Adjust the depth setting according to your skin thickness (typically 3-4 for average skin).

  3. 3

    Insert Test Strip

    Place a new Sinocare test strip into the meter. The device will automatically turn on and display a blood drop icon.
